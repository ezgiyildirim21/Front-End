ABOUT

There are many mountains in the world and the reason for their emergence is different. While some mountains are formed by the compression of the earth, some mountains are formed when lava rises to the surface and freezes. The source of volcanoes' lava is a very hot mass called magma. 54% of Asia, 36% of North America, 25% of Europe, 22% of South America, 17% of Australia and 3% of Africa It is covered with mountains. 24% of Earth's terrestrial mass is wholly mountainous. 10% of people live in mountainous regions. Most of Earth's rivers are fed by mountain sources, and more than half of humanity depends on mountains for water.

All mountains are not only on Earth. Other planets also have mountains. Examples of these are Mount Gila (3 km) on Venus and Olympus Mons (25 km) on Mars, the highest mountain in the Solar System, which covers almost half of Turkey. Apart from these, mountains 8 km high on the Moon and 18 km high on Mars can be given, but the measurement of the heights of these mountains is made from the surface of the planet and the mountains on Mars are extinct volcanoes. The highest peak in the Himalaya Mountains, the highest mountains in the world, is Mount Everest at an altitude of 8,850 meters.
